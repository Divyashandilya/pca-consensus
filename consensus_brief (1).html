Background & Unmet Need

Prostate cancer is the second most common cancer in Indian men, with incidence rising rapidly over the past two decades. Unlike Western populations, over 70% of Indian patients present at locally advanced or metastatic stage — driven by limited screening infrastructure, delayed healthcare-seeking, and access barriers. This late-stage presentation makes optimal treatment sequencing across the full disease continuum — from early referral and upstream disease management through to advanced metastatic disease — a clinical and public health imperative. The treatment landscape has evolved rapidly with novel hormonal agents, precision biomarker-driven therapies, and radioligand therapy (¹⁷⁷Lu-PSMA-617) transforming outcomes. No structured, India-specific expert consensus currently exists to guide clinicians on evidence-based pathways tailored to Indian clinical realities. This initiative addresses that critical gap.

Objectives & Scope

🎯

Primary Objective

  • First India-specific prostate cancer consensus — upstream to advanced disease
  • Standardise treatment pathways from early-stage referral to late-line mCRPC
  • Define where novel therapies including RLT fit within Indian practice
  • Encourage urologist, surgeon & oncologist participation
🔬

Scope of the Consensus

  • Upstream: PSA screening, early detection, referral pathways
  • mHSPC: treatment intensification — doublet and triplet therapy
  • mCRPC: sequencing — ARPIs, chemotherapy, PARP inhibitors, RLT
  • Biomarker-driven therapy: PSMA, BRCA1/2, HRR testing
  • India-specific: cost, access, BRIT/BARC supply, hub-and-spoke
📊

What Makes This Different

  • Addresses cost, Ayushman Bharat & generic availability

  • Designed for high-impact journal publication

Methodology — Three-Phase Modified Delphi Process

Phase 1

Broad Virtual Voting

~60 Experts

Initial consensus across all domains; diverse specialties including urologists and surgeons; identify agreement vs non-consensus items

Phase 2

Refined Virtual Voting

20–30 Experts

Focused voting on non-consensus items; narrowed expert panel with deeper disease and RLT expertise

Final ★

In-Person Meeting

10–15 Experts

Statement ratification, clinical nuance discussion, final publication sign-off and author agreement
